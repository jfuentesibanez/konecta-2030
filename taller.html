Build your first agent

In the next 110 minutes your team will go from chatting with an AI to directing an agent: giving it an objective, materials and a success criterion - and watching it work in a loop until it gets there.

The 3 rules of the workshop

1. Real work materials are welcome - your corporate license keeps everything inside Konecta's private workspace. Basic hygiene still applies: leave out customer personal data (names, phone numbers, IDs) - your agent doesn't need it to be impressive. Prefer a safe start? Fictional starter materials are ready in materials/.
2. Don't chase the perfect agent: chase the prototype that teaches something at demo day.
3. Stuck for more than 3 minutes? Hand up - that's what the facilitator is for.

01

Anatomy of an agent · shown in the live demo (15 min)

A chat answers questions. An agent pursues an objective. The difference is four pieces - the same four you will assemble today:

PieceWhat it isIn Cowork
ObjectiveWhat it must achieve, in one measurable sentenceThe first line of your brief
ContextThe materials it works withThe folder you point it at (docs, CSVs, notes)
ToolsWhat it can do: read, write files, produce documents - and reach other systems via connectorsBuilt in + connectors (section 02)
The loopWork → self-evaluate against the success criterion → fix → repeatYou ask for it in the brief (phase 3)

The piece that separates a toy from a tool is the fourth one: an agent without a success criterion doesn't know when it's finished - or when it's doing a bad job.

Build phases 1-2 with the folder only - capability multiplies scope creep. Add one connector in phase 3 if you're ahead of the clock. Which connectors are enabled on your machine depends on IT's setup - the room's support person has the list.

Worked example: the LIVE radar (web search)

Finished the Exercise A loop with time to spare? This is what a connector power-up looks like:

Power-up. You now have web search. TASK: for each of the 3 threats in your briefing, check whether the REAL CX/BPO market shows the same pattern right now. Find 1-2 recent news items per threat (last 6 months) about real companies, and add a "Reality check" section to the briefing: fictional threat vs real-world analogue, with URL and date for every claim. Keep the loop: same success criterion as before, now also requiring that every reality-check claim has a URL and a date. If you cannot find a real analogue for a threat, say so - that is a finding too.

The point of the exercise: the 5 notes were fictional - the pattern is not. Same idea for other teams: exercise B can turn its follow-up into a real mailbox draft (never send), exercise D can post its alert to the test channel. One connector, phase 3, nothing leaves the room.

03

Ideation · 20 min

Pick one exercise from the menu (or bring your own - rule 1 applies). Before touching a keyboard, fill in your team's Use-case card - it is also your demo-day script:

⬇ Use-case card (fillable PDF)   printed copies available in the room

The card asks for the four pieces plus connectors: objective · materials · connectors · deliverable · success criteria (binary!) · the loop · the bridge to Konecta.

The menu

A · The competitive radar Gentle

Give it 5 sector press notes (in materials/radar/) - or the web-search connector - and it produces a one-page briefing: the 3 most relevant threats to Konecta, why, and one signal to watch for each. Loop: it self-checks - does every threat cite evidence and carry an actionable signal? - and improves until it does.

B · The QBR analyst Gentle

Give it a client meeting transcript (fictional one in materials/qbr/, or a real one of yours - rule 1) and it produces: churn risks detected, commitments made with owners, and the draft follow-up email. Loop: a 5-point checklist it must cover; it reviews and fixes until all five are ticked.

C · The Kolibri case-maker Medium

Give it a described operational process (in materials/kolibri/, or one of your own) and it drafts the 11 steps of the Kolibri wizard (Basics → Owner → Problem → Impact → Data → Solution → Interface → Security → Readiness → Timeline → Review), deciding whether the case is deterministic, agentic or hybrid. Loop: it scores itself against the included rubric (10 criteria) and improves until ≥8/10. What comes out of this one can enter Kolibri for real tomorrow.

D · The early-warning watchtower Medium

Give it a market watchlist - 13 industry indicators, each with a threshold and a pre-agreed action (CSV in materials/senales/) - plus a package of "this quarter's news" (fictional). It decides which thresholds have actually been crossed and drafts the alert note to the committee: which indicators fired, which didn't (and how close they came), and which pre-agreed action is due. Loop: every conclusion must cite its news item and its threshold - otherwise it drops it and continues. Beware: the news package contains near-misses designed to punish rounding up.

E · The tough-customer sparring partner Bold

Build an agent that plays an angry customer (scenario script in materials/sparring/) to train responses, then scores the conversation against a Konecta-style quality rubric. Loop: the agent raises the difficulty when the response was good, lowers it when it wasn't. The most fun to demo.

04

Build · 75 min · 3 phases

1

Phase 1 · The brief (15 min) - the agent is born

Open Cowork (already installed and pointed at your team folder). Write your brief with this template - replace the brackets with your card:

You are our [AGENT NAME] agent. OBJECTIVE: [your one measurable sentence]. MATERIALS: work only with the files in the folder [materials/X - or your own files]. Do not invent data: if something is not in the files, say so explicitly. DELIVERABLE: [what it produces and in what format - e.g. a briefing.md document of one page maximum]. Start: read the materials, tell me in 5 lines what you understood and what your plan is before executing it.

⚠ Don't move on until the plan it proposes makes sense. Correct it in plain language - it's your new hire, not an oracle.

2

Phase 2 · First run (20 min) - the agent works

Give it the green light. Watch it read the files, reason, and write the deliverable into your folder. When it finishes, ask the two quality-control questions every good manager asks:

1. Which part of your deliverable has the weakest support in the materials? Be honest. 2. What did you lack to do this better?

That's already more than 95% of AI users ever do: demanding self-criticism before trusting the output.

3

Phase 3 · The loop (30 min) - the agent steers itself the star piece

Now turn it into a real agent: give it the success criterion and let it iterate on its own until it's met.

From now on you work in a loop until you meet this SUCCESS CRITERION: [your criterion - e.g. "each of the 3 threats cites evidence from the materials and carries one watchable signal"]. The loop: (1) evaluate your current deliverable against the criterion, point by point, scoring 0-10; (2) if anything scores below 8, fix it and re-evaluate; (3) repeat until everything is 8 or above, or for a maximum of 3 rounds. At the end, show me the evaluation table of every round so we can see how you improved.

Watch the loop run: evaluation → fix → re-evaluation. What you are watching - a system pursuing an objective and correcting itself - is the technology the 2030 projection after this workshop is about.

Ahead of the clock? Extras: (a) plug in ONE connector from section 02 and widen the agent's reach; (b) ask it to propose 3 improvements to its own brief (your Phase 1 prompt) and keep the best one. That's meta-optimization of agents - on your first day.

05

Why the loop matters

An LLM answers. An agent pursues. The difference isn't intelligence - it's architecture: objective + self-evaluation + iteration.

Everything you'll see in the 2030 projection after this workshop - where this loop meets 120,000 people and €2 billion of revenue - is the same architecture you just built, with more zeros on the budget. Keep your evaluation tables in mind when you get there: you'll recognize old friends.

06

Demo day · 30 min · ~5 min per team

Fixed format, timed, no slides - your filled Use-case card IS the script:

MinWhat you tell
0-1The card: objective, materials, connectors, deliverable, success criterion.
1-3Live demo: show the loop - the evaluation table improving across rounds is worth more than the deliverable itself.
3-4The confession: what did NOT work, or what surprised you. (Mandatory - half the learning lives here.)
4-5The bridge: if this were real at Konecta - which process, which account, who owns it? → direct candidate for the Kolibri wizard.

How the workshop ends

Every team's "bridge" goes on a visible list. Tomorrow, the ones worth it enter Kolibri through the 11-step wizard - the same one a team has been filling in with its agent today. The workshop doesn't end at demo day: it ends in the pipeline.

07

SOS - common blocks and the quick way out

SymptomWay out
"It can't find the files"Check Cowork is pointed at the team folder (visible in the window itself). If not: give it the exact folder path in the chat.
"It made up a fact"The golden rule: "that is not in the materials - go back to the files and cite where every claim comes from". Watching the agent correct itself is part of the workshop.
"The deliverable is generic, brochure-grade"Your success criterion isn't demanding enough. Add: "sentences any company could sign are forbidden - every point must cite a concrete fact from the materials".
"The loop doesn't converge / goes in circles"The criterion is ambiguous. Rewrite it as a binary checklist: met or not met. Max 3 rounds, then demo what you have.
"A connector isn't available"IT has the list of what's enabled per machine. No connector? Every exercise works folder-only - connectors are a power-up, not a requirement.
Anything elseHand up. Facilitator + IT support in the room.
