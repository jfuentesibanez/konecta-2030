Three ideas.

The three sentences to take home.
IDEA 1 / 3

The unit of this business is changing. For twenty years the industry sold hours of people; the market is starting to buy resolved outcomes.

What is being redistributed is not technology - it is pricing power. Whoever prices the new unit defines the new market.

IDEA 2 / 3

Capability is not deployed reality. The models can already do far more than enterprises actually run in production.

Everything valuable in the next four years happens inside that gap - and that gap is exactly the width of the window.

IDEA 3 / 3

The scarce asset is not intelligence - it is the right to operate it.

AI answers are heading toward free; owning the risk, the compliance and the trust is not. The business that survives sells accountability, with AI inside.

Five questions.

Only this committee can answer them. We leave them written - each with where to look.
QUESTION 1 / 5 · PRICING

In your next six renewals, who sets the price of an automated interaction - you, or the client's procurement?

Where to look: per-resolution clauses and savings-sharing demands in the 2026-27 renewal pipeline.

QUESTION 2 / 5 · CLIENTS

Which of your top-10 clients already has an internal conversational-AI team - and what will they still need you for in 2028?

Where to look: client job postings, QBR agendas, volumes quietly moving to self-service.

QUESTION 3 / 5 · PLATFORMS

When agents ship inside the licenses your clients already pay for, what exactly do they still buy from Konecta?

Where to look: the share of contracts where Konecta owns orchestration and data - not just the labor.

QUESTION 4 / 5 · PEOPLE

Can natural attrition outrun the transition - and what happens to the people who stay, handling only the hardest interactions?

Where to look: monthly attrition vs. the required reduction rate; pay, status and burnout in escalation teams.

QUESTION 5 / 5 · KOLIBRI

Is it a tool or a business - and would you sell it to someone who competes with your own operations?

Where to look: the "Sell" pipeline, and whether Kolibri has a price list by Q1 2027.

Seven trends.

Nobody controls them. Each one carries an observable red flag - when you see it, the report tells you what to do.
TREND 1 / 7

Outcome pricing goes mainstream.

$1.25 per resolution is already public.

Red flag: a top-10 client RFP admits per-resolution bids only.

TREND 2 / 7

Voice is getting solved.

<300ms latency, natural interruptions, any accent.

Red flag: a voice agent runs 1M+ retention calls/month at NPS parity.

TREND 3 / 7

Computer use dissolves the integration moat.

AI clicks through AS400/Citrix like a human - no APIs, no migration project.

Red flag: a client deploys agents on legacy systems with no integration project.

TREND 4 / 7

Platforms bundle agents as loss leaders.

40% license discounts already seen.

Red flag: automated interactions arrive "free" inside a major CCaaS renewal.

TREND 5 / 7

Customers start sending their own agents.

The contact center goes machine-to-machine.

Red flag: >2% of inbound identified as synthetic callers.

TREND 6 / 7

Regulation hardens into a moat.

AI Act, right-to-a-human, ISO 42001 in tenders.

Red flag: the first Tier-1 RFP disqualifies non-certified vendors.

TREND 7 / 7

The human premium becomes a product.

Klarna re-hired; paid human tiers are coming.

Red flag: a major brand charges extra for guaranteed human service.
